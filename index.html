Pachmarhi is a charming hill station nestled in the Satpura Range of Madhya Pradesh, India. This picturesque hill station, often hailed as the "Queen of Satpura," enchants visitors with its lush forests, cascading waterfalls, and panoramic viewpoints that offer breathtaking vistas of the surrounding landscape. With a rich history dating back to ancient times, Pachmarhi boasts of mythological significance intertwined with its rocky caves and tranquil valleys. From the sacred shrines of Chauragarh Temple and Mahadeo Cave Temple to the awe-inspiring Bee Falls and Dhoopgarh Peak, Pachmarhi beckons adventurers, nature enthusiasts, and seekers of solace alike, promising a memorable retreat amidst the timeless beauty of the Satpura Hills.

Jatashankar Temple

Jatashankar Temple in Pachmarhi is a special place where people go to pray and feel connected to God. It's a cave temple nestled in the mountains, dedicated to Lord Shiva, one of the important gods in Hinduism. Inside the temple, there's a natural rock formation that looks like Shiva's matted hair, which is why it's called "Jatashankar." People believe that visiting this temple brings peace and blessings. The surroundings are calm and serene, making it a peaceful spot for meditation and reflection. Many visitors also enjoy exploring the caves and enjoying the beautiful nature around the temple.
